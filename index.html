About Me

Thirty years in the trenches has taught me that good governance isn't about perfect processes—it's about creating spaces where people can do their best work with integrity and purpose.

Leadership Philosophy

I've learned that the best governance happens when we stop seeing it as a checkbox exercise and start understanding it as the foundation for trust, accountability, and better outcomes. It's messy work, but it's also some of the most important work we can do.

Whether I'm sitting around a boardroom table as a Non-Executive Director, advising on complex legal matters as General Counsel, or helping organisations navigate safeguarding challenges, my approach stays the same: listen deeply, ask the right questions, and help people see the bigger picture.

I'm particularly passionate about creating governance frameworks that support vulnerable people and drive gender equity. Because at the end of the day, good governance should make the world a little bit better for everyone.
